Jollof Rice

Jollof Rice is a West African dish with origin in Senegal. The food is eaten for various occasions and it a very tasty meal that is relatively easy to make.

Ingredient:

  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1 medium plum tomato
  • 1/2 medium red onion
  • 1 inch piece of fresh ginger
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable or peanut oil
  • 1/2 medium red onion
  • 1/4 cup of tomato paste
  • 2 cups of rice
  • 1 1/2 cup of curry powder
  • 1 bouillon cube
  • 2 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups of chicken stock

Below is a recipe to cook a good bowl of Jollof Rice:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 Fahrenheit

  2. Prepare the pepper stew base

    Add bell peppers, tomato, onion, scotch bonnet or habanero pepper, ginger, and garlic into a blender until smooth. Set it aside.

  3. Fry the onions

    Heat the oil and add onions to fry for 2-3 minutes.

  4. Cook the tomato paste

    Add the tomato paste and cook it for 4 to 5 minutes, while stirring frequently.

  5. Cook the pepper stew base

    Carefully stir in the pepper stew base and cook it uncovered for 20 to 25 minutes until reduced to half. Occasionally stir with a wooden spoon.

  6. Add the rice and seasonings

    Stir in the rice, curry powder, bouillon cube, dried thyme, garlic powder, onion powder, nutmeg, bay leaves, and salt. Cook for 3 minutes and frequently stir. Add chicken stock and bring it to a simmer.

  7. Bake the Jollof Rice

    Turn the heat off and tightly cover the pot with foil and a lid. Transfer the pot into the preheated oven and bake, undisturbed for about 40 minutes.

  8. Serve

    Gently stir the rice. Discard the bay leaves and transfer the rice to a platter. Serve warm for the best taste.
